# 03 – Patient Triage

> “Sort fast, save smart.”

This section defines how Atlas RP EMS uses color‑coded triage to decide who gets treated and transported first during any incident, from single‑patient calls to large‑scale scenes.[web:4][web:44]

---

## 3.1 Triage Overview

Triage is the process of quickly classifying patients based on how urgently they need care.[web:39][web:44]  
We use a four‑color system:

- **Red** – Immediate  
- **Yellow** – Delayed  
- **Green** – Minor  
- **Black** – Deceased / Expectant  

Triage is about **prioritizing**, not deciding who “matters” more; it keeps scenes organized and believable while still respecting player stories.[web:4][web:44]

---

## 3.2 Color Codes and RP Meaning

### 3.2.1 Red – Immediate

> “If you don’t help them now, they die.”

**Examples (RP):**  
- Massive bleeding, obvious severe trauma, unresponsive with weak breathing.  
- GSW to chest/neck, major burns, severe head injury, major car wreck with bad presentation.[web:4][web:44]

**EMS Approach:**  
- First priority for hands‑on care and transport.  
- At least one clear treatment sequence before any revive/heal script.  
- Red patients usually get transported, not treated and left on scene.

---

### 3.2.2 Yellow – Delayed

> “Serious, but they won’t die in the next minute.”

**Examples (RP):**  
- Fractures, moderate bleeding controlled with bandaging, stable GSW to extremity.  
- Conscious but in pain, stable vitals, no obvious immediate life threat.[web:4][web:44]

**EMS Approach:**  
- Treat after Reds are stabilized.  
- Can wait briefly for transport if resources are limited.  
- Still deserve solid RP, but may have fewer time‑critical interventions.

---

### 3.2.3 Green – Minor

> “They’ll walk themselves out if they have to.”

**Examples (RP):**  
- Walking wounded, minor cuts/abrasions, bruises, small burns.  
- Patient talking clearly, moving around, complaining more than collapsing.[web:4][web:44]

**EMS Approach:**  
- Lowest priority in multi‑patient incidents.  
- Can be directed to a “casualty collection” spot while EMS focuses on Reds/Yellows.  
- Great targets for short, efficient RP so you can stay available for serious calls.

---

### 3.2.4 Black – Deceased / Expectant

> “Beyond what we can realistically do here.”

**Examples (RP):**  
- No pulse, not breathing, obvious catastrophic injury incompatible with life.  
- Situations where the player explicitly wants their character to be dead, following server rules.[web:4][web:44]

**EMS Approach:**  
- Confirm death RP‑wise using basic checks and emotes.  
- Inform PD/Fire/Coroner as appropriate.  
- Do not spam revives; respect server death/permadeath policies and the player’s story.[web:4][web:40]

---

## 3.3 Triage in Practice

### 3.3.1 Basic Triage Flow

On any scene with more than one patient:

1. **Scan** – Quick visual check: who looks worst at a glance.  
2. **Tag** – Assign a mental color (or verbal: “Red male, Green female”).  
3. **Act** – Treat Reds, then Yellows, then Greens.  
4. **Update** – Colors can change as conditions improve or worsen.[web:44]

Keep your triage decisions simple and clear; you are not doing real‑world algorithms, just recognizable MedRP.[web:4][web:25]

---

### 3.3.2 RP Examples

Use /me and /do to make triage obvious to everyone:

/me does a quick scan of the scene, checking who is breathing and bleeding the worst.
/me kneels by the unconscious male, checks for a pulse and breathing.
/do You would see a red triage tag placed on your wrist.
/me walks over to the standing female and gives her a green tag, telling her to wait by the ambulance.

### 3.4 Single‑Patient Triage

Even when there is only one patient, you should still quickly decide where they would land on the color scale. This helps you pace your RP and decide how aggressive you should be with treatment and transport.

- Treat a clearly critical patient as **Red**: move fast, focus on life‑saving interventions, and get them moving toward a hospital rather than over‑roleplaying on scene.  
- Treat a clearly injured but stable patient as **Yellow**: give them solid care and RP, but you have a little breathing room.  
- Treat a minor complaint as **Green**: keep it light and efficient so you can stay available for real emergencies.

Example:

- Unconscious after a high‑speed crash with bad injuries → effectively Red.  
- Broken arm, controlled bleeding, talking clearly → effectively Yellow.  
- Small cut, no real impairment, just wants a bandage → effectively Green.

---

### 3.5 Multi‑Agency Triage

When PD or Fire are involved, triage is a team sport, but EMS owns the medical side.

- **PD** handles scene safety, suspects, weapons, and traffic control.  
- **Fire/Rescue** (if present) may handle extrication, fire suppression, and can assist with initial triage.  
- **EMS** has final say on medical triage categories and transport priority unless a staff member directs otherwise.
