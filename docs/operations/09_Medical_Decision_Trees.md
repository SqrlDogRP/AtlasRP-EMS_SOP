# Operations — Medical Decision Trees (Consistency Layer)

These are *decision aids* so outcomes are consistent and arguments are rare.

## General rule
Mechanism + severity + down time determine outcome.

### A) Gunshot Wounds (GSW)
**Treat & Release (rare):**
- Grazing wound, stable, no down time, believable minor impact.

**Transport required:**
- Any torso GSW
- Any multiple GSW
- Any significant blood loss / altered mental status

**Surgery likely (AMU):**
- Multiple GSW
- Torso GSW with instability
- Prolonged down time

**Catastrophic / non-survivable (possible):**
- Extreme context (high-caliber, prolonged down time, massive trauma)
- EMS/AMU may declare non-survivable in good faith

### B) Stabbing / Cutting
**Treat & Release:**
- Superficial lacerations, stable, minimal blood loss

**Transport required:**
- Deep wounds, abdominal/chest involvement, heavy bleeding

**Surgery likely:**
- Chest/abdomen penetration with instability

### C) Motor Vehicle Collisions (MVC)
**Treat & Release:**
- Low-speed, minimal vehicle damage, stable exam

**Transport required:**
- High-speed, rollover, ejection, loss of consciousness

**Severe:**
- Multiple injuries, unstable vitals, major mechanism

### D) Falls
**Treat & Release:**
- Low height, no LOC, stable

**Transport required:**
- Significant height, head strike, LOC, neuro symptoms

### E) Overdose / Poisoning
**Transport required:**
- Any decreased consciousness
- Unknown substance
- Repeat overdose in short window

**Release possible after evaluation:**
- Fully alert, stable, monitored, and cleared by EMS/AMU as appropriate

### F) Explosions / Burns
**Transport required:**
- Any significant burns, smoke inhalation, or blast injury concerns

### Outcome conflict rule
If a patient disputes outcome: escalate to Supervisor/Command and keep the scene moving.
