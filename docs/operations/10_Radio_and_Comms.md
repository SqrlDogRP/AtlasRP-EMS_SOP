# Radio and Communications Protocol

**Document ID:** 10_Radio_and_Comms  
**Division:** Emergency Medical Services (EMS)  
**Effective Date:** April 2026  
**Applies To:** PD, EMS, DOJ Personnel  

---

## Overview

Radio channels 1–10 are encrypted and strictly job-bound. These channels ensure secure, coordinated communication among emergency services while maintaining clear separation between departments unless explicitly permitted.

---

## Channel Guide

Radio Channels 1–10 are encrypted and job-bound. Here are the designated channels for PD, EMS, and DOJ:

| Channel | Designation               | Access            | Description |
|--------:|---------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| CH 1    | PD Main Channel           | PD, EMS           | Primary dispatch and coordination channel for the Police Department. EMS may access for active scenes or shared operations. |
| CH 2    | PD Secondary Channel      | PD Only           | Used for tactical coordination, pursuits, and special operations. |
| CH 3    | PD Overflow Channel       | PD Only           | Backup channel for overflow or large-scale responses. |
| CH 4    | PD Overflow Channel       | PD Only           | Reserved for major incidents requiring additional PD separation. |
| CH 5    | PD Overflow Channel       | PD Only           | Additional backup for PD-only comms or field coordination. |
| CH 6    | EMS Main Channel          | EMS, PD           | Core EMS dispatch and operations. PD may access for medical assistance or joint responses. |
| CH 7    | EMS Secondary Channel     | EMS Only          | Used for internal EMS coordination, patient transport, and private field updates. |
| CH 8    | DOJ Main Channel          | DOJ Only          | Department of Justice internal operations and logistics. Not accessible by other departments. |
| CH 9    | Emergency Services Channel| PD, EMS, DOJ      | Shared emergency coordination channel for multi-agency responses and courtroom communication when necessary. |
| CH 10   | PD Private Channel        | PD Only           | Encrypted private channel for high-level police communications. EMS and DOJ have no access. |

---

## Usage Protocol

- **Professional conduct:** All transmissions must remain concise, professional, and free from unnecessary chatter.  
- **Clarity first:** Identify your unit and location before giving or requesting information.  
  - Example: `Medic-2 to Dispatch, en route to Vespucci incident.`  
- **Cross-department communication:** Use joint-access channels only for verified shared operations (e.g., active scenes, major emergencies, coordinated transports).  
- **Channel switching:** Units must monitor their designated primary channels unless authorized to switch. Always notify dispatch when changing frequencies.  
- **Encryption security:** Do not share encrypted channel details, frequencies, or access credentials with unauthorized personnel.

---

## Interoperability

- **PD and EMS collaboration:** EMS may temporarily utilize PD Main (CH 1), and PD may access EMS Main (CH 6) during shared medical or tactical situations when operationally necessary.  
- **DOJ coordination:** DOJ primarily operates on CH 8 but may use CH 9 for court-related or emergency briefings.  
- **Court sessions:** CH 9 doubles as the designated court channel when judicial operations are in session and radio coordination is required.

---

## Chain of Command and Compliance

- **PD radio oversight:** Police Command or Dispatch Supervisor.  
- **EMS radio oversight:** EMS Chief, Deputy Chief, or on-duty Supervisor.  
- **DOJ radio oversight:** Attorney General, Chief Judge, or designated Court Communications Lead.  

Violations of radio protocol may result in communication restrictions, mandatory retraining, or disciplinary action in accordance with the respective department’s SOP and disciplinary guidelines.

---

## Quick Reference

- Stay on your primary channel unless directed otherwise.  
- Identify unit, location, and intent in your first transmission.  
- Use CH 9 for multi-agency emergencies and court operations.  
- Keep comms clear, brief, and mission-focused.
