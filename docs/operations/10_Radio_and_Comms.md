Radio channels (1–10) are encrypted and strictly job-bound. These channels ensure secure, coordinated communication among emergency services while maintaining clear separation between departments unless explicitly permitted. Cross-communication must always follow appropriate chain-of-command and operational necessity.

Channel Guide
Channel	Designation	Access	Description
CH 1	PD Main Channel	PD, EMS	Primary dispatch and coordination channel for Police Department. EMS may access for active scenes or shared operations.
CH 2	PD Secondary	PD Only	Used for tactical coordination, pursuits, and special operations.
CH 3	PD Overflow	PD Only	Backup for overflow or large-scale responses.
CH 4	PD Overflow	PD Only	Reserved for major incidents requiring additional PD separation.
CH 5	PD Overflow	PD Only	Additional backup for PD-only comms or field coordination.
CH 6	EMS Main Channel	EMS, PD	Core EMS dispatch and operations. PD may access for medical assistance or joint response.
CH 7	EMS Secondary	EMS Only	Used for internal EMS coordination, patient transport, and private field updates.
CH 8	DOJ Main Channel	DOJ Only	Department of Justice internal operations and logistics. Not accessible by other departments.
CH 9	Emergency Services	PD, EMS, DOJ	Shared emergency coordination channel. Used for multi-agency responses and courtroom communication when necessary.
CH 10	PD Private Channel	PD Only	Encrypted private channel for high-level police comms. EMS and DOJ have no access.
Usage Protocol
Professional Conduct: All transmissions should remain concise, professional, and free from unnecessary chatter.

Clarity First: Identify your unit and location before giving or requesting information.
Example: “Medic-2 to Dispatch, en route to Vespucci incident.”

Cross-Department Communication:
Use joint-access channels only for verified shared operations (e.g., active scenes, major emergencies).

Channel Switching:
Units must monitor their designated primary channels unless authorized to switch. Always notify dispatch when changing frequencies.

Encryption Security:
Do not share encrypted channel details, frequencies, or access codes with unauthorized personnel.

Interoperability Notes
PD and EMS Collaboration: EMS may temporarily utilize PD Main (CH 1) or PD may access EMS Main (CH 6) during shared medical or tactical situations.

DOJ Coordination: DOJ primarily operates on CH 8 but may enter CH 9 for court-related or emergency briefings.

Court Sessions: CH 9 doubles as the designated Court Channel when judicial operations are in session.

Chain of Command for Radio Oversight
PD Radio Oversight: Police Command or Dispatch Supervisor

EMS Radio Oversight: EMS Chief or On-Duty Supervisor

DOJ Radio Oversight: Attorney General or Court Communications Lead

Violations of radio protocol may result in communication restrictions, retraining, or disciplinary action as per department guidelines.
