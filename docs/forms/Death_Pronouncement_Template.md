### Death Pronouncement Template (RP)

**Step 1 – Confirm death (IC / /me style)**  
- /me checks for a carotid pulse and spontaneous respirations.  
- /me listens for heart sounds for 60 seconds.  
- /me checks pupils for response to light.  
- /me notes no response to verbal or painful stimuli.  

**Step 2 – Call time of death (radio / local)**  
- “Control, Medic [###]. Be advised, resuscitation efforts have been terminated.  
  Patient is deceased. Time of death [HH:MM].”  

**Step 3 – Speak to family / bystanders (plain, clear words)**  
- “I’m so **sorry**. We’ve done everything we could for them, but they have died.”[web:21]  
- “There is nothing more we can do to bring them back.”[web:21]  
- “You can stay with them for a moment if you’d like.”[web:20][web:26]  

Avoid: “They passed away,” “They’re in a better place now,” or jokes/IC sarcasm. Be direct but compassionate.[web:16][web:26]  

**Step 4 – Explain briefly what was done (1–2 lines)**  
- “We gave medications, performed CPR, and used a defibrillator, but their heart never started beating again.”[web:21]  

**Step 5 – Next‑steps RP (depends on scene type)**  
- If at home: “Police and the coroner will help with arrangements from here.”[web:24]  
- If in public: “We’ll secure the area and coordinate transport to the hospital.”[web:24]  

**Optional /note format (for medical chart RP)**  
- “Pronounced deceased at [HH:MM] by [Unit/Name]. No pulse, no respirations, no heart sounds, pupils fixed and dilated, no response to stimuli.”[web:22][web:28][web:30]
