---

#### C – Chief Complaint

- Patient age/sex and main complaint in 1 line:  
  - “34 y/o male c/o chest pain since 20 minutes PTA.”  
- Where was the patient found and in what position?  

---

#### H – History

**History of Present Illness / Injury:**  
- Onset (when it started), provocation, quality, radiation, severity, time (OPQRST if you use it).  
- What was the patient doing when it began?  
- Mechanism of injury if trauma (MVC, fall, GSW, etc.).  

**Pertinent Medical History:**  
- Past medical history (PMH).  
- Medications.  
- Allergies.  
- Pertinent negatives (denies SOB, denies LOC, etc.).[web:45][web:52]  

---

#### A – Assessment

**Initial General Impression:**  
- “Alert but anxious,” “unresponsive,” “in obvious respiratory distress,” etc.[web:40]  

**Vitals (with times):**  
- Time / BP / HR / RR / SpO2 / Temp / Pain / GCS.  

**Physical Exam (head‑to‑toe or focused):**  
- Neuro: LOC, orientation, pupils, deficits.  
- Airway / Breathing: airway status, breath sounds, work of breathing.  
- Circulation: skin color/temp, pulses, major bleeding.  
- Specific findings: deformities, tenderness, swelling, wounds, etc.  
- Pertinent negatives (no chest wall tenderness, no JVD, no abdominal pain, etc.).[web:45][web:54]  

---

#### R – Rx / Treatment

List treatments in **order performed**, with times and responses:[web:45][web:40]  
- Oxygen (type, flow rate, response).  
- Positioning, splinting, spinal precautions.  
- Medications (name, dose, route, time, response).  
- Defibrillation/CPR details if applicable.  
- Reassessments (vitals after each major intervention).  

---

#### T – Transport

- Patient condition during transport (improved / unchanged / worsened).  
- Mode of transport (code level, lights/siren or no).  
- Destination and why chosen (closest, specialty center, patient preference).  
- Handoff:  
  - “Full report given to RN/MD at [Hospital] including history, assessment, treatments, and response. Care transferred at [time].”[web:42][web:40]  

---

#### Narrative (One Flowing Paragraph or 2–3 Short Ones)

Write this like a story in order of events:[web:40][web:57]  

- Dispatch & arrival: what you were sent for, how/where you found the patient.  
- Assessment: key findings and pertinent negatives.  
- Treatments: what you did and why you chose those interventions.  
- Transport: patient response, changes, and final condition at handoff.  

Example starter:  
- “Unit M3 dispatched code 3 for 34 y/o male with chest pain at home. On arrival, patient found sitting on couch, alert and oriented x4, clutching chest…”
