# 11. Life Flight Manual

> "When ground can’t get there in time, we take the sky."

**Applies to:** Atlas RP EMS – Air Medical (Life Flight)  
**Initial Status:** Locked role – Chief EMS is the only authorized pilot until further notice.

---

## 11.1 Purpose & Overview

Life Flight provides rapid air medical transport for critically ill or injured patients when ground units are too slow, unsafe, or unavailable.[page:1]  

Goals:  
- Stabilize and transport critical patients as fast and safely as possible.  
- Add high‑quality, realistic RP without overshadowing ground EMS or LEO.  
- Keep air operations tightly controlled to avoid chaos, trolls, or abuse.

---

## 11.2 When Life Flight Can Be Used

Life Flight is for **high-acuity** calls only, not routine taxiing of patients.[page:1]  

Use Life Flight when:  
- The scene is far from a hospital (long ground travel).  
- Terrain or route is dangerous (mountains, water, hostile areas).  
- Multiple critical patients and limited ground units.  
- Command specifically requests air because of scene conditions.  

Do **not** use Life Flight for:  
- Simple injuries that can ride in an ambulance.  
- “Because it looks cool” with no medical/scene justification.  
- Personal transport or joyriding.

---

## 11.3 Requesting Life Flight (Ground Unit Steps)

Any on‑scene EMS or LEO may request Life Flight; it is **approved or denied** by the on‑duty Chief Pilot (currently the EMS Chief).[page:1]  

1. **Size‑up first**  
   - Confirm scene is safe or has an active LEO perimeter.  
   - Identify patient count and severity (e.g., 1 critical GSW, unstable).  

2. **Radio format (example)**  
   - “Dispatch, Medic 3 requesting Life Flight to [location], [brief reason], [LZ status].”  
   - Example: “Dispatch, Medic 3 requesting Life Flight to Route 68 airstrip, 1 critical trauma, secure LZ.”  

3. **Provide details when asked**  
   - Exact location / landmark.  
   - Number of patients and priority.  
   - Hazards (wires, trees, gunfire, weather RP).  

4. **Wait for approval**  
   - Chief Pilot responds with “Life Flight accepted” or “Life Flight declined, use ground.”  

---

## 11.4 Life Flight Pilot Responsibilities

Only authorized Life Flight pilots may fly the helicopter; at launch, this is **EMS Chief only**.[page:1]  

Pilot must:  
- Ensure the aircraft is spawned, fueled (RP), and visually checked.  
- Confirm call details with Dispatch before liftoff.  
- Fly safely: no stunt flying, low‑altitude showboating, or ignoring airspace obstacles.  
- Choose a safe LZ and wave off if unsafe.  
- Maintain calm, clear comms with Dispatch and ground units.  

---

## 11.5 Scene & Landing Zone (LZ) Management

A safe LZ is mandatory; ground units manage it, pilot has final say.[page:1]  

**Ground unit LZ requirements:**  
- Flat, open area roughly the size of a small parking lot (about 30x30 in game).  
- Clear of vehicles, pedestrians, debris, and live gunfire.  
- No overhead wires, low bridges, or tall trees directly over the landing spot.  

**Ground unit LZ duties:**  
- Mark the LZ using vehicles, flares, or lights (RP, don’t blind the pilot).  
- Keep all civilians and non‑essential units out of the rotor area.  
- Maintain perimeter until Life Flight departs.  

**Pilot LZ duties:**  
- Conduct one fly‑over if needed to visually check the LZ.  
- If unsafe, call “Go‑around, LZ unsafe” and request a new LZ or cancel.  
- Land only when comfortable with the approach and surroundings.

---

## 11.6 Patient Handoff & Transport

Once on the ground, time is critical but RP is still important.[page:1]  

**Ground to air handoff:**  
- Ground medic gives a quick report: mechanism, vitals, interventions, and current status.  
- Agree on destination hospital (or specialized facility if your server uses them).  

**On board:**  
- Confirm how many can ride: normally patient + 1 medic; extras only if safe and approved by pilot.  
- Use brief /emotes or /me to RP ongoing care (ventilation, IVs, monitoring).  

**Arrival at hospital:**  
- Pilot lands in designated helipad/LZ.  
- Air crew transfers patient to ED staff or ground EMS with a short handoff report.  

---

## 11.7 Certification & Training Requirements

Life Flight is a **restricted certification**. Initially, only the EMS Chief holds this certification.[page:1]  

To become Life Flight certified, a member must:

1. **Meet baseline requirements**  
   - Be a fully cleared EMS provider (no probation).  
   - Demonstrate strong scene control, radio discipline, and medical RP.  

2. **Complete ground training module**  
   - Review this Life Flight Manual in full.  
   - Demonstrate understanding of when to call Life Flight vs ground.  
   - Show proper LZ setup and radio request in a training scenario.  

3. **Complete flight observer rides (in RP)**  
   - Minimum of 3 Life Flight calls as an observer with the Chief Pilot.  
   - Ask questions, learn routes, and observe LZ decisions and comms.  

4. **Complete supervised pilot flights**  
   - Minimum of 3 flights where trainee is pilot under Chief Pilot supervision.  
   - Evaluated on: takeoff/landing, LZ selection, comms, RP quality, decision making.  

5. **Final sign‑off**  
   - Chief Pilot documents completion and grants “Life Flight Pilot” tag/role on Discord and in‑game if applicable.  
   - Only then may the member fly Life Flight without direct supervision.  

---

## 11.8 Operating Rules & Limitations

To keep Life Flight special and controlled:[page:1]  

- Life Flight only spawns with authorization from EMS Command (or Chief Pilot).  
- Only certified pilots may sit in the pilot seat and move the aircraft.  
- Life Flight does not self‑dispatch; it must be requested by a unit or Command.  
- Max concurrent Life Flight aircraft: 1, unless Command explicitly approves more.  
- If server performance, chaos, or event load is high, Command may ground Life Flight temporarily.

---

## 11.9 Disciplinary & Safety Considerations

Because Life Flight involves extra power and visibility, misuse is taken seriously.[page:1]  

**Examples of misuse:**  
- Flying for fun with no call.  
- Reckless flying, crashing repeatedly, or intentionally ramming.  
- Ignoring LZ safety and landing in obviously unsafe spots.  
- Using Life Flight for non‑medical transport (friends, loot, etc.).  

**Consequences (at EMS Command discretion):**  
- Verbal/written warning.  
- Temporary suspension of Life Flight privileges.  
- Full revocation of Life Flight certification.  
- Possible EMS removal or staff action for severe behavior.

## 11.10 Future Expansion

As the server grows, Life Flight can expand:[page:1]  

- Additional certified pilots and dedicated flight medics.  
- Night ops / weather RP rules if your server uses them.  
- Inter‑facility transfers (ICU to trauma center) if hospitals are differentiated.  

All changes will be logged in the EMS Changelog and announced in the EMS Discord.
