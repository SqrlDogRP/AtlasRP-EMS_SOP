# 04 – Radio Communications on EMS Scenes

> “Key up, be clear, get off the air.”

This section explains how Atlas RP EMS should use the radio before, during, and after scenes. The goal is simple, brief, and clear communication that keeps dispatch, PD/Fire, and other EMS units on the same page.[web:50][web:53][web:63]

---

## 4.1 Core Radio Rules

- **Think before you talk.** Know what you’re going to say before you key up.[web:50][web:63]  
- **Keep it short.** Use a few clear words, not long speeches.[web:53][web:57]  
- **One person at a time.** Don’t talk over others; listen first.[web:53][web:60]  
- **Identify yourself.** Use your unit name at the start of each transmission.[web:50][web:59]  
- **Stay professional.** No trolling, screaming, or OOC chatter on operational channels.[web:50][web:53]

Basic format:

> **Receiver, this is Sender, message.**

Example:

> “Dispatch, Medic 1, en route to last 911.”  
> “Medic 1, Dispatch, go ahead.”

---

## 4.2 Standard Status Messages

Use consistent phrases for your status so dispatch and other units always know what you’re doing.[web:50][web:53][web:63]

Recommended:

- **En Route** – You’re responding to a call.  
  - “Dispatch, Medic 2 en route to Vespucci Boulevard call.”  
- **On Scene** – You’ve arrived at the incident.  
  - “Dispatch, Medic 2 on scene.”[web:49][web:50]  
- **Patient Contact** – You’ve made contact with the patient(s).  
  - “Medic 2 to Dispatch, patient contact with one male.”  
- **Transporting** – You’re leaving with a patient.  
  - “Medic 2 transporting one Red priority to Pillbox, ETA 3 minutes.”[web:50]  
- **At Hospital / At Destination** – You’ve arrived with the patient.  
  - “Medic 2 at Pillbox.”[web:50]  
- **Back In Service** – You’re ready for the next call.  
  - “Medic 2 back in service.”

Avoid extra filler like “be advised” or “arriving on scene” unless it adds information.[web:63]

## 4.3 Scene Arrival and Updates

### 4.3.1 First Unit on Scene

The first EMS unit on scene should:

1. Announce arrival.  
2. Give a short initial report once they’ve quickly sized up the scene.[web:50][web:63]

Example:

```text
"Dispatch, Medic 1 on scene."

[After quick look:]

"Dispatch, Medic 1: multiple vehicles, three patients visible, PD on scene, EMS establishing medical command."
