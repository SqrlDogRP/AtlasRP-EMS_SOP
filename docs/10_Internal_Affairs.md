# 12. Internal Affairs Manual

> "We protect the integrity of the department, not just its image."

**Applies to:** Atlas RP EMS – All Members  
**Initial Status:** Internal Affairs (IA) is a command‑level function led by the EMS Chief and designated IA Officers.

---

## 12.1 Purpose & Overview

Internal Affairs exists to investigate allegations of misconduct, policy violations, and serious performance issues within EMS.[web:4]  
The goal is to keep EMS fair, professional, and consistent, while protecting both the department and its members from false or exaggerated claims.[web:4]  

Internal Affairs is **not** about witch hunts or OOC drama; it is an IC/IC‑adjacent system to:  
- Ensure members follow SOPs and server rules.  
- Provide a clear path to report concerns.  
- Apply discipline fairly and consistently.

---

## 12.2 What Internal Affairs Handles

IA deals with **serious** allegations that go beyond a simple coaching talk. Examples include:[web:4][web:5]  
- Abuse of EMS powers (force‑reviving, meta‑gaming, intentionally ignoring calls).  
- Harassment, discrimination, or targeted trolling of other players.  
- Repeated insubordination or refusal to follow chain of command.  
- Major breaches of SOP (drug abuse on duty, gross negligence, etc.).  

IA usually **does not** handle:  
- Minor IC disagreements or simple misunderstandings.  
- Basic performance issues that can be fixed by training/FTO.  
- OOC rule breaks that are clearly a staff‑only issue (handled by server staff).  

Command may move a case to IA if a pattern of behavior develops or if the impact to the department is large.

---

## 12.3 Who Can File a Complaint

Any of the following may file an IA complaint:  
- EMS members of any rank.  
- Other departments (LEO/Fire/Comms) through their leadership.  
- Server staff bringing serious EMS concerns forward.  

Anonymous complaints may be accepted, but may be harder to fully investigate if there is limited evidence.

---

## 12.4 How to File a Complaint

Preferred method (example):  
- Open a ticket in the EMS Discord using the **Internal Affairs** ticket option, or  
- Contact IA/Command via the designated IA form/channel.  

When filing, include:  
- Your in‑game name and Discord tag (or note if you want to remain anonymous to the accused).  
- The accused member’s name and callsign if known.  
- Date/time and location (approximate is fine).  
- What happened (short narrative).  
- Any evidence: clips, screenshots, logs, witnesses.  

IC complaints made on‑scene can be noted, but IA will still require a follow‑up ticket or written statement for tracking.

---

## 12.5 IA Roles & Responsibilities

**EMS Chief / Head of IA**  
- Oversees all IA investigations.  
- Assigns IA Investigators to cases and approves final outcomes.  

**IA Investigator(s)**  
- Collect statements, review evidence, and document findings.  
- Remain neutral, professional, and avoid personal bias.  
- Make recommendations to Command (sustain, not sustain, or other resolution).  

**Chain of Command (Captains, Lieutenants, etc.)**  
- Handle minor issues informally when possible.  
- Forward serious or repeated issues to IA with a brief summary and any evidence.

---

## 12.6 Investigation Process

Internal Affairs follows a simple, transparent process modeled on real‑world standards but adapted for RP.[web:4][web:15]  

1. **Intake & logging**  
   - IA receives the complaint and logs it with a case number and date.  
   - IA determines if it is truly an IA case or better handled as training/coaching.  

2. **Notification**  
   - The accused member is notified that they are under IA review (unless secrecy is needed for a short time due to active evidence gathering).[web:15]  
   - They are told the general nature of the allegation.  

3. **Evidence gathering**  
   - IA collects: clips, logs, screenshots, radio recordings (if any), and witness statements.  
   - Both the complainant and the accused will have a chance to provide their side.  

4. **Analysis & findings**  
   - IA compares behavior to SOPs, server rules, and expectations.  
   - IA decides whether the allegation is:  
     - **Sustained** – supported by evidence.  
     - **Not Sustained** – some evidence, but not enough to be sure.  
     - **Unfounded** – no evidence it happened.  
     - **Exonerated** – it happened, but actions were within policy.[web:4]  

5. **Recommendation & action**  
   - IA recommends actions to EMS Command (training, warning, suspension, removal, or no action).  
   - EMS Command makes the final decision and documents the outcome.  

6. **Closeout**  
   - IA closes the case, notes the outcome, and notifies relevant parties within reason (protecting privacy where needed).  

---

## 12.7 Member Rights & Expectations

Members under IA review have rights, including:[web:4][web:15]  
- The right to know they are under investigation (except in rare cases where a short delay is needed).  
- The right to tell their side of the story and present evidence.  
- The right to be treated respectfully and fairly during the process.  

They also have responsibilities:  
- Be honest and complete in their statements.  
- Avoid retaliating against anyone who filed or participated in the complaint.  
- Follow any temporary restrictions put in place (e.g., removed from command or FTO roles during review).

---

## 12.8 Possible Outcomes & Discipline

Based on the findings, IA/Command may impose one or more of the following:  
- **Coaching / Training:** For minor or first‑time issues where education is enough.  
- **Verbal or Written Warning:** Documented notice that behavior must change.  
- **Role / Permission Restrictions:** Removal from FTO, Command, Life Flight, or other special units.  
- **Temporary Suspension:** Time‑limited removal from EMS or from certain shifts/roles.  
- **Removal from EMS:** For severe or repeated misconduct.  
- **Referral to Server Staff:** If actions also break server rules requiring staff punishment.  

IA cases found to be unfounded or exonerated may still lead to training or feedback if communication or expectations were unclear.

---

## 12.9 False or Malicious Complaints

Filing an IA complaint in **good faith**, even if it is not sustained, is never punishable.  
However, knowingly filing **false or malicious** complaints to harass or attack another member may itself become an IA case and result in discipline.  

---

## 12.10 Confidentiality & Records

IA cases are treated as **confidential** and are only discussed between involved leadership, IA staff, and server staff as needed.[web:4]  
Case logs may be kept in a private IA/Command channel or document, accessible only to designated leadership.  

Public posting or gossip about ongoing IA cases is discouraged and may result in separate discipline if it harms the department or individuals.

---

## 12.11 IA and Roleplay

Internal Affairs should support RP, not kill it.  
When possible, IA outcomes are framed in character (e.g., “administrative leave,” “written reprimand in file”) while respecting OOC boundaries and server staff decisions.  

Players are encouraged to bring concerns to IA instead of arguing IC/OOC, rage‑quitting, or starting drama in public channels.

---

## 12.12 Quick Reference Checklist

**For Anyone Filing an IA Complaint:**  
- Is this serious enough for IA (abuse, harassment, major SOP break)?  
- Gather evidence (clips, screenshots, names, times).  
- Open an IA ticket or form with clear, short details.  

**For IA / Command Handling a Case:**  
- Log the case and assign an investigator.  
- Notify the accused (when appropriate).  
- Collect statements from all sides.  
- Compare actions to SOPs and rules.  
- Decide on sustained / not sustained / unfounded / exonerated.  
- Apply fair, consistent action and close the case.

---

All changes to Internal Affairs procedures will be approved by EMS Command and noted in the EMS Changelog and Discord announcements.
