# Definitions & Acronyms

This document defines official terminology used within the ATLAS RP Emergency Medical Authority (AEMA).

All members are expected to understand and operate within these definitions.

If terminology conflicts arise, this document serves as the authoritative reference.

---

# I. Organizational Terms

**AEMA**  
ATLAS Emergency Medical Authority. The governing body for EMS operations on ATLAS RP.

**EMS**  
Emergency Medical Services. All certified medical personnel operating under AEMA.

**AMU (Advanced Medicine Unit)**  
Hospital-based advanced providers responsible for surgery, medical holds, recovery decisions, and complex medical outcomes.

**CRU (Crisis Response Unit)**  
Specialized personnel trained for mental health crisis intervention and de-escalation.

**FTO (Field Training Officer)**  
Certified member responsible for evaluating and training new providers.

**IA (Internal Affairs)**  
Administrative oversight division responsible for investigating misconduct, scope violations, and policy abuse.

**Command**  
Director, Deputy Director, Chief Medical Officer (CMO), and Lieutenant.

**Supervisor**  
Lead Paramedic, Senior Paramedic, or designated acting supervisor.

---

# II. Operational Codes

**Code 1**  
Routine response. No lights or sirens.

**Code 2**  
Urgent response. Lights only. Due regard required.

**Code 3**  
Emergency response. Lights and sirens. Due regard required.

**Code 4**  
Scene secure. Safe for EMS entry.

**Staging**  
Holding position at a safe distance until scene security is confirmed.

**MCI (Mass Casualty Incident)**  
An incident involving multiple patients that exceeds normal single-unit capacity and requires structured incident command.

---

# III. Medical Documentation

**PCR (Patient Care Report)**  
Official documentation of patient assessment, treatment, and disposition.

**RMA (Refusal of Medical Aid)**  
Formal documentation of a competent patient declining treatment and/or transport.

**Death Pronouncement**  
Official declaration of non-survivability made by authorized personnel.

**Medical Hold**  
Temporary restriction placed by AMU requiring a patient to remain under medical supervision due to instability or safety concerns.

---

# IV. Scope & Authority

**Scope of Practice**  
The defined level of care a provider is authorized to perform based on certification.

**Good Faith Medical Decision**  
A clinical decision made using reasonable judgment based on scene context, injury severity, and established SOP guidance.

**Medical Outcome**  
Immediate survivability and stabilization decisions made by EMS on scene.

**Hospital Outcome**  
Post-transport decisions made by AMU including surgery, restrictions, and recovery timelines.

**Recovery Restriction**  
Temporary limitation placed on a patient’s activity following significant trauma.

**Strike Points**  
Documented disciplinary points assigned for policy violations under the Strike System.

---

# V. Injury Classification System

**Minor Trauma**  
Injury unlikely to cause long-term impairment. Limited recovery RP required.

**Moderate Trauma**  
Significant injury requiring transport and temporary recovery limitations.

**Severe Trauma**  
Life-threatening injury requiring transport and likely surgical intervention.

**Catastrophic Trauma**  
Extreme injury with potential non-survivability or long-term impairment.

---

# VI. Scene Control & Safety

**Scene Security**  
Law Enforcement responsibility to ensure the environment is safe for EMS operations.

**Disengagement**  
Mandatory withdrawal from a scene due to active violence or loss of security.

**Unsafe Scene**  
Any environment where active threats exist or Code 4 has not been declared.

---

# VII. Enforcement Language

For clarity, the following terms carry weight:

**MUST** – Required action. Non-compliance may result in discipline.  
**MUST NOT** – Prohibited action. Violation may result in discipline.  
**SHOULD** – Strong recommendation based on best practice.  
**MAY** – Permissible but not required.

---

# VIII. Policy Conflict Rule

If two documents appear to conflict:

1. The most recently updated document in the index governs.
2. Command will issue clarification if required.
3. Definitions in this document override informal interpretations.
